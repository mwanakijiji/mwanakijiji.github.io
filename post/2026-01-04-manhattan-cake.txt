Edith Warner was a figure who lived with her companion TBD, of the nearby San Ildefonso Pueblo. Robert Oppenheimer had stopped by her café during his jaunts in the area, before he picked the nearby Los Alamos Boys School as a candidate site for The Project.

The café at Otowi Bridge was an important place where the scientists, engineers, and other staff could come together to socialize in an area, and under circumstances of secrecy, where few such possibilities existed.

Oppenheimer and other scientists were said to be particularly enamoured of her chocolate cake, and it became famous ...

To my knowledge, there are two extant recipes of said cake. One appears in an appendix in the book *The House at Otowi Bridge: The Story of Edith Warner and Los Alamos*, by Peggy Pond Church. The other is in *In the Shadow of Los Alamos: Selected Writings of Edith Warner*, edited by Patrick Burns. Here they are, where I have made some tiny changes for clarity and presentation, and neglect Church's additional tips for baking at high altitude.

### Edith Warner's Chocolate Loaf Cake (version found in Burns)
*Source: Patrick Burns,* *In the Shadow of Los Alamos: Selected Writings of Edith Warner*


#### Ingredients for the loaf:
- 1½ ounces chocolate
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 1 cup flour, sifted three times
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 2 teaspoons baking powder (or 1 teaspoon double-acting baking powder)
- ½ cup milk
- 3 eggs (unbeaten)

#### Directions:
2. Melt chocolate and butter together over hot water.
3. Pour the above over the sugar
4. Add the flour, salt, and baking powder, and sift 3 times.
5. Add the milk.
6. Beat for 2 minutes with a rotary egg-beater.
7. Add 3 *unbeaten* eggs.
8. Beat for 2 more minutes.
9. Pour into a greased pan, preferably a loaf pan.
10. Bake in a slow oven, gradually raising the heat to 300°F, for 1 hour or until done. *(A shallow pan requires a shorter baking period.)*

#### Ingredients for the chocolate frosting:
- 1¼ cups powdered sugar
- 2 heaping tablespoons cocoa powder
- Pinch of salt
- 2 tablespoons butter
- Hot coffee

#### Directions:
1. Melt and pour the above [CHECK] 2 tablespoons butter
2. Add hot coffee until consistency for spreading.

Slight variations (of the first? recipe) can be found on LANL websites.

There is a downside to these recipes, though: they contain a lot of butter, sugar, and TBD, and is not at all healthy. Of course one cannot expect a chocolate cake to be healthy, but the recipes can stand a few minor tweaks to make them less appalling.

For that, I'll use 

- monkfruit instead of sugar 
- olive oil instead of butter
- eggs whites only, without egg yolks

I would be remiss not to add that there is more that one can do to simulate a Manhattan Project staffer's eating-out. Church writes, "The meals that Edith served were always simple: a stew flavored with herbs on big terra-cotta Mexican plates; posole, an Indian dish made of parched corn; lettuce in a black pottery bowl; fresh bread; a sweet tomato relish, watermelon and pickle, spiced peaches or apricots; a dessert of raspberries." Burns also reprints recipes of hers for spicy gingerbread and oatmeal bread. Try them out!
